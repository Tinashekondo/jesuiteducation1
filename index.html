OUT COMES

The Core Values of Jesuit education facilitate the formation of school graduates who are people of:

Competence:

Jesuit Education forms individuals with the ability to do a job properly, indicating a combination of knowledge, attitude, skills and behaviour used to improve performance. Jesuit Education aims at forming individuals who are qualified to perform a specific roles, and can do the job in thoroughness in a wide variety of situations. Persons of Competence apply interdisciplinary approaches to solving problems, think critically and creatively, and expresses themselves proficiently in both oral and written communication. They regularly update, seek constructive criticism and take others' help and support for their continual growth.

Conscience:

In a Jesuit school, learners are encouraged and developed to lead a life in harmony with ethical universal principles such as sharing, forgiveness, integrity, truthfulness and respect for life and our environment. Persons of conscience discerns what is right, good and true and have the courage to do it. They practice the habit of reflection and enjoys communion with God. Their conscience encourages valuing inter-religious collaboration and respect for the faith and cultures of diverse religious communities. Their decisions and actions are in consonance with principles of justice and a better life for all.

Compassion:

It denotes solidarity and empathy to those in greatest need. Persons of compassion manifest a preferential love for the poor and disadvantaged of the society. They serve others and find fulfilment in actions that affirm the dignity of every person as a son and daughter of God. They actively engage themselves in the life of the local and global community.

Commitment:

It denotes one's willingness and determination to give one's time, talents and expertise for a task or a cause that brings a positive change in oneself, in others and in society. This requires openness to one's growth, and an understanding of the roots of unfairness in our social institutions. Persons of Commitment advocate for social changes that assist the poor, marginalised and vulnerable to regaining their human dignity.

ST IGNATIUS PRAYERS

(ST. IGNATIUS OF LOYOLA)

“TAKE, LORD, AND RECEIVE ALL MY LIBERTY,| MY MEMORY, MY UNDERSTANDING AND MY ENTIRE WILL, ALL I HAVE AND CALL MY OWN. YOU HAVE GIVEN ALL TO ME. TO YOU, LORD, I RETURN IT. EVERYTHING IS YOURS; DO WITH IT WHAT YOU WILL. GIVE ME ONLY YOUR LOVE AND YOUR GRACE. THAT IS ENOUGH FOR ME.”

ANIMA CHRISTI
(ST. IGNATIUS OF LOYOLA)

“ SOUL OF CHRIST, SANCTIFY ME BODY OF CHRIST, SAVE ME BLOOD OF CHRIST, INEBRIATE ME WATER FROM THE SIDE OF CHRIST, WASH ME PASSION OF CHRIST, STRENGTHEN ME GOOD JESUS, HEAR ME WITHIN THE WOUNDS, SHELTER ME FROM TURNING AWAY, KEEP ME FROM THE EVIL ONE, PROTECT ME AT THE HOUR OF MY DEATH, CALL ME INTO YOUR PRESENCE LEAD ME TO PRAISE YOU WITH ALL YOUR SAINTS FOREVER AND EVER AMEN”

PRAYER FOR GENEROSITY
(ST. IGNATIUS OF LOYOLA)

“ LORD, TEACH ME TO BE GENEROUS. TEACH ME TO SERVE YOU AS YOU DESERVE; TO GIVE AND NOT TO COUNT THE COST, TO FIGHT AND NOT TO HEED THE WOUNDS, TO TOIL AND NOT TO SEEK FOR REST, TO LABOR AND NOT TO ASK FOR REWARD, SAVE THAT OF KNOWING THAT I DO YOUR WILL.”
